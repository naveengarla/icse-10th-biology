# Section 2.1: What Are Chromosomes?

## 🔎 The Hidden Suitcases
If you look at a normal, living cell under a standard microscope without staining it, you won't see much inside the nucleus. 
But if you add special dyes, a magical network of extremely thin, dark-staining threads appears. 

👉 **Textbook Term:** This loose, messy network is called **Chromatin Fibres**.

However, when the cell gets ready to divide (in the very first stage called *Prophase*), these super-thin chromatin fibers violently condense, coil up, and form thick, highly visible structures.
These are **Chromosomes**. 

🔥 **Memory Trick (The Name):** 
Because they greedily soak up the dyes we add, scientists named them Chromosomes:
- **Chroma** = Coloured 
- **Soma** = Body
So, they are literally just "Coloured Bodies"!
