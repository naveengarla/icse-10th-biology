# Section 2.2: Discovery of Chromosomes

## 🔬 The Man Who Saw the Threads (Walther Fleming)
Who actually discovered these "coloured bodies"? 

In **1882**, a German scientist named **Walther Fleming** was studying the rapidly dividing cells of a salamander larva (an amphibian). 
Using his old-school microscope, he noticed tiny threads that appeared to be dividing directly down the middle, lengthwise. 

Because they looked like little threads, he gave this process of division a famous name: **Mitosis**.
- **Mitos** literally means "thread" in Greek.

So, whenever you say "Mitosis", you are just speaking Ancient Greek for "Thread-process"!
