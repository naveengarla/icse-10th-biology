# Section 2.4: What Are Genes?

## 🧬 Understanding the Master Blueprint
In the previous section, we learned that DNA is a massive, twisting double-helix made up of nucleotides. 

But what does it actually *do*?

👉 **Textbook Definition:** **Genes** are specific sequences of nucleotides on a chromosome that encode particular proteins, which express in the form of some particular feature of the body.

Think of it this way:
- If a chromosome is an encyclopedia of your body's instructions...
- Then a **Gene** is a single sentence inside that book that codes for exactly one feature (like your eye color, the shape of your nose, or the enzymes in your stomach).

---
### 🧬 A Note on DNA Fingerprinting (Not in Syllabus, But Cool!)
*Your textbook mentions this as an extra fun fact:*
About 99% of your DNA is actually non-functional! However, these non-functional areas show tremendous variation from person to person. 
Scientists can look at these variations to identify individuals. This is called **DNA Profiling** or **DNA Fingerprinting**. It is used in criminal cases to match blood or hair back to a suspect, or to establish maternity/paternity!
