# Section 2.5: New Cells Need to Be Produced

## 2B. CELL DIVISION - NEW CELLS FROM EXISTING ONES

Why does cell division even happen? Why go through all the trouble of mitosis and meiosis? 
Your textbook lists **4 main reasons** why new cells must be produced:

## 🌱 1. For Growth
Every organism, whether a plant or an animal, begins its life as a single cell (the fertilized egg). 
This egg divides repeatedly to form a cluster, which eventually shapes into tissues and organs. A 4-day-old human embryo already contains 16 cells (from $1 \to 2 \to 4 \to 8 \to 16$).

## 🏎️ 2. For Replacement
There is always wear and tear in your normal body functions. 
- *Crazy Fact:* **20 million red blood cells** in our body are destroyed every single minute! 
They must continually be replaced by new cells formed by parents in the bone marrow. 
In plants, old dried leaves fall off and new ones grow out.

## 🛠️ 3. For Repair
Apart from normal wear and tear, what if you get into an accident? 
If you get a cut on your skin or fracture a bone in your arm, the cells around the injury simply divide to cover the gaps and join the broken ends.

## 👶 4. For Reproduction
Reproduction happens through the activity of dividing cells. 
- Amoeba and bacteria just blindly divide to produce two similar independent cells (Mitosis). 
- In higher forms like humans, the reproductive organs undergo a special kind of cell division (**Meiosis**) to produce sperms and eggs with exactly half the normal number of chromosomes!
