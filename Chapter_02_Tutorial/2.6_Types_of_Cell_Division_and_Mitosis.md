# Section 2.6: Types of Cell Division & Mitosis

There are exactly two types of cell division:
1. **Mitosis:** Cell division leading to growth and development.
2. **Meiosis:** Cell division leading to the production of gametes (sex cells).

## 2.6.1 MITOSIS (Mitos: thread)
Mitosis splits one parent cell into **two identical daughter cells**. 
The most crucial rule to remember: **The exact same normal chromosome number is maintained at each division.** 

Mitosis is split into two major phases: Karyokinesis (splitting the nucleus) and Cytokinesis (splitting the cytoplasm).

---
### 🎭 Phases of Mitosis — Karyokinesis
It occurs in 4 continuous phases. 
🔥 **Memory Trick:** **P**lease **M**ake **A**nother **T**aco!

**1. Prophase (pro = first)**
- Chromosomes become short, thick, and clearly visible. 
- They have already duplicated into sister chromatids attached at the centromere.
- In animal cells, the centrosome splits and the **centrioles** move apart to opposite 'poles'. Radiating rays form an **aster** (star) around them.
- Spindle fibres appear. The **nuclear membrane and nucleolus disappear**.

**2. Metaphase (meta = after)**
- The duplicated chromosomes magically arrange on the **equatorial plane** (the very middle of the cell).
- Each chromosome gets attached to a spindle fibre by its centromere.

**3. Anaphase (ana = up, back)**
- The centromere divides. The two sister chromatids snap apart!
- They are rapidly drawn apart toward opposite poles by the contraction (shortening) of the spindle fibres. A furrow starts in the membrane (in animal cells).

**4. Telophase (telo = end)**
- The chromatids (now called daughter chromosomes) reach opposite poles. They thin out into a network of chromatin threads.
- Spindle fibres disappear. 
- **The nuclear membrane and nucleolus magically reappear!** 

---
### ✂️ Cytokinesis (Division of Cytoplasm)
At the end of telophase, the cell body itself must split. 
- **In Animal cells:** A **cleavage furrow** appears in the middle and deepens until it pops the cell into two.
- **In Plant cells:** A **cell plate** is laid down right down the middle, growing from the centre to the periphery.

## 2.6.2 Differences in Mitosis in Animal and Plant Cells

| 🐶 Animals | 🌳 Plants |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asters** are formed. | **Asters are not formed.** |
| Cytokinesis by **furrowing of cytoplasm**. | Cytokinesis by **cell plate formation**. |
| Occurs in most tissues throughout the body (for growth/replacement). | Occurs mainly at the **growing tips** (for lengthening) and sides (for girth). |

## 2.6.3 Significance of Mitosis
1. **Growth** (increase in body size).
2. **Repair** (healing wounded tissues).
3. **Replacement** (replacing old/dead cells like skin epidermis and RBCs).
4. **Asexual Reproduction** (amoeba dividing into two).
5. **Maintains same chromosome number** in the daughter cells!
