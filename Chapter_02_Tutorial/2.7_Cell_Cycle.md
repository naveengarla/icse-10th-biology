# Section 2.7: Cell Cycle ("Divide, grow and redivide")

At the end of Mitosis, the new cells are incredibly small with a full-sized nucleus but very little cytoplasm. 
They don't just stay that way. They immediately enter **Interphase**—historically called the "resting phase", but actually it is the most active period of the cell's entire life!

During Interphase, the cell prepares for the *next* division. Interphase has exactly three phases:

## 🧭 The 3 Phases of Interphase
**1. First Growth Phase ($G_1$)**
- RNA and proteins are heavily synthesized.
- The volume of cytoplasm increases.
- Mitochondria (in all cells) and chloroplasts (in plant cells) quietly divide (because they have their own DNA!).
- *Note:* In late $G_1$, cells can either start preparing for division, or **withdraw permanently** into a resting phase ($R$).

**2. Synthesis Phase ($S$)**
- **More DNA is synthesized and chromosomes are duplicated!** This means if the cell starts with 46 strings of DNA, it builds a photocopy for all 46 strings so it will be ready to split in the future.

**3. Second Growth Phase ($G_2$)**
- This is a shorter phase. More RNA and proteins necessary for division are pumped out. Now the cell is fully ready to enter Mitosis, and the cycle repeats.

---
## ⏳ How long does the cell cycle go on?
Can cells just divide endlessly forever? 
**No!** At some places it stops permanently, at some temporarily, and at others till needed.

| Type of Cell | How often do they divide / get replaced? |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Brain / Nerve Cells** | **Never!** Once formed in the embryo, they do not divide further. Once dead, they are never replaced. |
| **Surface Skin Cells** | Continuously lost and replaced. In fact, a large portion of household dust is dead human skin! Replacements happen every **two weeks**. |
| **Red Blood Cells** | Replaced roughly every **120 days**. |
| **Liver Cells** | Replaced only once every **300 to 500 days** (usually just to replace destroyed cells). |
| **Bone Cells** | Replaced every **10 years** or so in adults. |

*(In plants, division happens incredibly rapidly, but only at specific locations called **meristems**).*
